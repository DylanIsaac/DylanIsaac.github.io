<p>As an alum and someone who has volunteered at MIT for years, I will offer this perspective.  MIT does not have any bonus points or considerations for legacy, you have to earn that spot on merit.</p><p>However, that being said, a son or daughter of an MIT graduate is more than likely to have picked up a way of thinking and doing things from that parent that will often be something that is consistent with the MIT way, thus making you a potentially better fit.  Since MIT gets thousands more qualified applicants than they can accept each year, it comes down to things like fit.</p><p>I know a number of my fellow alums had kids apply and none of them got in.  Others had kids who had ZERO interest in going there, even if they might have been accepted.  (They did go to other highly competitive schools like the Ivys.)   Kids probably also have a better idea of what MIT means beyond the logo from just hearing their parent(s) talk about school.</p><p>Now this is not unique to MIT, but any school will probably find kids of alumni a slightly better fit on average due to how they were raised and what the kid was exposed to all those formative years.</p>
